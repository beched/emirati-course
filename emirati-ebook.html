0 · The sound code

Emiratis chat in the Latin alphabet, using numbers for the Arabic sounds that English has no letter for. This book prints both, so the numbers are just a pronunciation crutch — here is the whole code.

CodeLetterHow to say it
7حa hard H pushed from the throat
3ع‘ayn — a tight “swallowed” vowel from deep in the throat
ghغa gargled French R
khخthe ch in Bach
th / dhث / ذthink / this
shشship
gقEmirati: the letter qaf becomes a hard G — gool (say), gahwa (coffee)
chك → چEmirati: some K softens to CH — esh7alech (how are you, to a woman)
yج → يEmirati: some J becomes Y — yedeed (new), yaahil (kid)
The one rule that saves you. If you already know some Arabic (or Arabic loanwords in your language), just soften it: q→g, j→y, k→ch, and let the hard letters relax. Emirati is Arabic with the edges filed off.

Vowels & stress

A doubled vowel is long — hold it; a single vowel is short.

letterread as
a / aashort а / long а̄ (baab = door)
i, eshort — a clipped и (closer to и than э; leans to э only next to the throat letters 7, 3, gh, q)
eelong и, as in “see” / мир — yedeed, gareeb
o / ooshort о / long у as in “moon” / суп — roo7, shoof
Stress: land it on the long vowel (aa / ee / oo), usually the last one — a-ROO7, mash-GHOOL, ta3-BAAN, BAA-cher. If a word has no long vowel, stress the first syllable — MAR-7aba, KA-tab. Arabic stress is lighter than in Russian; clean consonants matter more than perfect stress.

2 · Saying NOT · having things

Two “not”s

mub negates a noun or adjective. ma negates a verb. Never mix them up.

أنا مُبْ تَعْبانْ
ana mub ta3baan
I'm not tired
ما أعْرَفْ
ma a3ref
I don't know

8 · How words are built from a root

This is the deep secret of Arabic — and it lets you guess and even invent words. Almost every word grows from a 3-consonant root poured into a fixed pattern. Take the root k · t · b (the idea of “writing”):

WordArabiziPattern
كِتابْkitaab — bookfi3aal (a thing)
كاتِبْkaatib — writerfaa3il (the doer)
مَكْتَبْmaktab — officemaf3al (place of)
مَكْتوبْmaktoob — written / a lettermaf3ool (the done)
يِكْتِبْyiktib — he writesyif3il (present verb)

See the machine? Now the general patterns. ف ع ل (f‑3‑l) are the stand-in slots for “root letter 1, 2, 3”:

PatternArabiziWhat it makes
فاعِلfaa3ilthe doer — kaatib (writer), laa3ib (player)
مَفْعولmaf3oolthe done — maktoob (written), mashghool (busy)
مَفْعَلmaf3althe place — maktab (office), mal3ab (pitch), matbakh (kitchen)
فَعّالfa33aala profession — khabbaaz (baker), tabbaakh (cook)
فْعيلf3eelan adjective — kbeer (big), gareeb (near), ladheedh (tasty)
تَفْعيلtaf3eelthe act of doing — tadreeb (training), tanZeef (cleaning)
You do not need to build these from scratch to speak. But once you notice the pattern, whole families of words unlock at once — sakan (to live) → saakin (resident), maskan (dwelling); daras (to study) → madrasa (school), mudarris (teacher). 9 · Importing English words

Nobody knows every word. Emiratis borrow English constantly, and so can you. Four rules turn any English word into something an Emirati will understand instantly:

  1. Swap the sounds you lack. There is no P (→B) and no V (→F): laptop → labtob, video → fidyo. A hard G stays G.
  2. Make it definite with el-. el laptop, el meeting, el gym.
  3. Pluralize with -aat. laptop → labtob-aat, file → fail-aat.
  4. Verb it with asawwi (“I do”). asawwi download, asawwi update; future = basawwi download.
عِنْدي ميتينق الحِيْنْ، بَسَوّي أبْدِيتْ بَعْدَيْنْ
3indi meeting el7een, basawwi update ba3dain
I have a meeting now, I'll do an update later

★ One-page cheat sheet

Be: nothing. ana ta3baan = I'm tired.

Not: mub + noun/adj · ma + verb.

Have: 3ind + ending — 3indi / 3indak / 3indah / 3indha / 3indna / 3indhum.

Want / like / must / can: aba / a7ebb / laazim / agdar (+ noun or verb).

There is / isn't: fee / ma fee.

Present: a-(I) t-(you/she) y-(he/they) n-(we). Future: add b-. Past: -t = I/you did.

This/that/the: haadha / haadhi · hal-noun · dhaak / dheech.

Questions: shoo · minoo · wain · meta · laish · chaif · kam · ay (first in the sentence).

Glue: wo (and) · bas (but) · 3ashaan (because) · fa (so) · wo ba3dain (and then) · wiya (with) · elly (which).

Import English: P→B, V→F · add el- · plural -aat · verb it with asawwi.
